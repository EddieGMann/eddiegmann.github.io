One of the few opportunities to go on main campus!

The Learning Technology Program Coordinator is responsible for the following:

  • Genome Academy Events
  • Sending out Qualtric sign-ups, finding a room, confirming dates with faculty, advertising and requesting JV vouchers get sent out.
    • Qualtrics
      • Name, NetID, Email, which workshop they would like to attend, and then if you want you can ask about position (staff, faculty, student, post-doc)
      • The threat about $100 dollar no-show fee is hollow. Life happens. Sometimes people forget they sign-up, sometimes a nervous presentor does a bad job in the first of a two-part seminar and no one wants to come to the second. No need to ask for fund code. In fact, a presentor asked us to not include it.
    • Finding a room and confirming dates.
      • Please see sidebar for information about possible locations
      • I usually show up with an HDMI cable, laptop adaptors and my own Duke laptop
      • Reuben-Cooke 133's door handle never turns, just push to open. Also bring any extension cords that you can find.
      • FCIEMAS has two doors, both of which are generally locked. Contact or find someone to open. They will ask if you tried both doors. Try not to glare.
      • French Science 4233 has a laptop AV hookup in the corner. If you can find a clicker to use, bring it along.
      • Dr Steve Rozen has the inside track on a room in Trent Semans (4067). He has become very active with the academy.
      • No one wants to use Hock 214 or 10089 or the 11th floor classrooms, despite them being very nice. Keep offering.
      • Advertising
        • Ask Alissa Kocer to send out information to mailing list. This will include an announcement that looks very similar to the previous descriptions on the website. Ask all the program coordinators you know that work with science if they will send the announcement to their mailing list.
        • Requesting JV vouchers
          • Honorarium is $50 dollars, with a goal of 5 workshops a semester.
          • Email the finance person with the name of presentor, the code(get from presentor)and workshop details. That information is required by the Duke system
